Network Scheduler and Priority Lanes

← Back

Opensplice DDS offers a DDS Network Scheduler to organize and classify data based on importance and urgency, creating network channels for each urgent and important data stream class.

These channels are priority lanes. They help pre-empt less urgent data streams and allocate network bandwidth to the highest-priority, most up-to-date, and most urgent data streams as defined by their QoS.

The Network Scheduler can create as many network priority lanes as needed and helps enforce message priority even on non-priority preserving transports such as TCP/IP or UDP/IP.
